PRODUCTS

Vigan Longganisa

Description:Vigan Longganisa is a garlicky, tangy, and slightly salty native sausage made from ground pork and native Ilocano spices. It's distinct for its strong vinegar and garlic flavor and is often served with rice and egg (longsilog). These sausage are handmade and often sold in bundles in local markets.

price:PHP 150/dozen

Bagnet

Description:Bagnet is deep-fried crispy pork belly, similar to lechon kawali but cooked twice for extra crunch. It is a popular Ilocano delicacy known for its golden-brown skin and juicy meat. Often paired with sukang Iloko (native vinegar), It's favorite pasalubong.

price:PHP 650/kilo

Sukang Iloko

Description:Sukang ILoko is a dark, fermented sugarcane vinegar with a distinct tangy and smooky flavor. It's a stapple in Ilocano households and is used as a dipping sauce or cooking ingredient. Aged for months, it develops a rich, complex taste.

price:PHP 100/350ml bottle

Tupig

Description:Tupig is a grilled native rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and strips of young coconut, wrapped in banana leaves. It has a smoky, sweet flavor and chewy texture. Commonly sold during festivals and roadside stalls, it’s a favorite Ilocano merienda..

price:PHP 15/piece

Inabel (Abel Iloko)

Description:Inabel is a handwoven textile made from cotton or traditional fibers, known for its durability and intricate patterns. It is used to make blankets, scarves, table runners, and clothing. A symbol of Ilocano heritage, it supports local weaving communities..

price:PHP 2,000

Chicharon (cornick)

Description:Chichacorn is crunchy, partially popped corn kernels seasoned with various flavors like cheese, garlic, or spicy chili. It's a favorite snack in Ilocos Sur for its addictive crunch and taste. It’s also a popular pasalubong sold in resealable packs..

price:PHP 60/100g pack

Royal Bibingka

Description:Royal Bibingka is a sticky, sweet rice cake made with glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and cheese, baked until golden. Unlike the soft bibingka of other provinces, it has a chewy texture and caramelized top. It's especially popular during holidays and fiestas..

price:PHP 200/small box

Tinubong

Description:Tinubong is a native delicacy made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar, cooked inside bamboo tubes. The process gives it a smoky flavor and unique presentation. Traditionally made during special occasions, it makes for a memorable Ilocano treat..

price:PHP 60/tube

Burnay Jars

Description:Burnay jars are traditional earthenware pots made from clay and sand, used for fermenting fish, vinegar, or storing water. Handcrafted using age-old pottery techniques, they are both functional and decorative. These jars are icons of Vigan's craftsmanship.

price:PHP 2,000

Damili

Description:Damili refers to the art of making red clay pottery like pots, figurines, and decorative items. The tradition is strong in Ilocos Sur, especially in the town of San Nicolas. These products reflect both utility and heritage..

price:PHP 1,500

Vigan Empanda

Description:Vigan Empanada is a deep-fried snack filled with grated green papaya, mung beans, egg, and Vigan longganisa, encased in a thin rice flour wrapper. It’s a must-try street food in Vigan, often served with sukang Iloko. Its vibrant orange color and crispy shell make it visually appealing too..

price:PHP 6p/piece

Peanut Brittle

Description:Ilocos peanut brittle is a sweet, crunchy snack made of roasted peanuts coated in caramelized sugar. Often homemade, it is sold in tightly sealed jars or packs as a classic pasalubong. It balances sweetness and nuttiness perfectly..

price:PHP 100/Jar
